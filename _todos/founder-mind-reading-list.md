# Reading List: Founder Psychology & Inner Growth

Short, targeted reads mapped to the exact challenges and insights we've explored.

---

## 1. Procrastination & Inner Resistance

**The War of Art** by Steven Pressfield

- Read: Book One – “Resistance” (~40 pages)
- Why: Helps you understand why your brain resists the work you _know_ you need to do. Cuts straight into the psychology of avoidance.

---

## 2. Perfectionism & Fear of Being "Not Enough"

**The Gifts of Imperfection** by Brené Brown

- Read: Chapter 2 – Cultivating Self-Compassion
- Why: Shatters the myth that perfectionism is a strength. Helps you build self-trust even when your work feels messy or incomplete.

---

## 3. Founder Clarity & Overthinking

**Make Something for Someone** by Paul Graham

- Read: Full essay (5–10 min)
- Why: Refocuses your energy from self-image and anxiety to actual users and usefulness.

---

## 4. Founder Identity Without Losing Yourself

**Anything You Want** by Derek Sivers

- Read: Full book (~1 hour total)
- Why: Shows you how to build true to yourself. Reinforces that success doesn't need to feel like self-sacrifice or burnout.

---

## 5. Emotional Integration & Surrender

**Letting Go** by David R. Hawkins

- Read: Introduction + Chapter 1
- Why: Helps you release emotional blocks like shame, fear, and craving — without needing to rationalize or suppress them.

---

## 6. Systems Thinking & How America Designed Leverage

**The Sovereign Individual** by James Dale Davidson & Lord William Rees-Mogg

- Read: Introduction + Chapter 1
- Why: Frames how modern systems (like America) optimize for control, productivity, and adaptation — connects directly to our talk on capitalism, leverage, and lock-in.

---

## 7. Founder Psychology Around Money & Ego

**The Psychology of Money** by Morgan Housel

- Read: Any standalone chapters (3–5 pages each)
- Why: Makes you rethink how wealth, fear, and self-worth are connected — in a way that’s readable and emotionally grounded.

---

## 8. Hunger for Connection While Building

**The Missing Piece in Hustle Culture** by Justin Welsh

- Read: Short essay + LinkedIn thread (~5 min)
- Why: Reflects your emotional reality — that building alone isn't enough. You need love, meaning, and connection to feel whole.
