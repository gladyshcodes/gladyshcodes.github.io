## 'What' to do?

When running a business, it's critically important to allocate resources properly. Especially if there's a scarcity of them. As a solo founder of `Ray (AI QA)`, I have periodically but consistently made a mistake of diving into something that might not be worth pursuing at all.

- Before doing anything, ask yourself, "Must we do this?"
- 'Must' then scope, then build.
- The default answer is always a strong No.
- Do something that is absolutely necessary.
- Your success won't be measured by slight improvements or nice-to-haves; it will be measured by the significant impact you make.
- No one will care about your minor projects if your startup fails—and failure is more likely to happen if you don't focus on the right things.

Practically:
- Stend some time upfront to evaluate the required input versus the potential output.
  - Take worst scenario input size.
- Calculate your leverage index, defined as the ratio of the amount of output to the amount of input.
- Proceed only if the leverage index is considerably high.
