## 'What' to do?

When running a business, it's critically important to allocate resources properly, especially if there's a scarcity of them. As a solo founder of Ray, I have periodically but consistently made a mistake of diving into something that might not be worth doing at all

If there's binary decision:

- Default answer is 'No'
- Do something if it's absolutely necessary
- Do not spend time on slight improvements, we are looking for big change
- You can think of slight improvements later on, when the product gets traction; no one is going to care about your 'small change' neither when you're presenting your users with your 'Innovative' product, nor when it turns out not to be what people need
- Carefully evaluate input and output, calculate the potential leverage index, and act if and only if it's extremely high in your view
